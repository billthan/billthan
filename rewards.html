UofT kiosks needs a reward system.

THE PROBLEM

Current kiosk systems are slow and inefficient. Kiosk workers are not confidently serving customers.

OUR SOLUTION

We have created a proto-persona. REWARDS is designed for University cafeteria management.

Lets define our problem further...

DEFINITIONS

"Users" - The Users of REWARDS
"Customers" - The Clients of the Users
"T-Card"- The identification card for Customers
"Points" - The virtual currency applied to User Accounts

ASSUMPTIONS

All Customers are registered with UofT
All Customers are have T-Cards and ID numbers
Only Users can use the REWARDS system

My Role

This project was closely worked on by Selin Sabircan and myself under the supervision and guidance of Dr. St Cyr at the University of Toronto. The process of solution brainstorming, low/high fidelity usability testing methodology, and high fidelity mocking was jointly addressed by Selin and myself.

Lessons Learned

  • Efficiency matters. We identified the root of UofT kiosk and remedied it by designing for user efficiency.
  • Accessbility is key to efficiency. The thoughtfully picked colours used in REWARDS reflect consistency and standards heuristics.
  • Consistency familiarizes unfamiliar users. New Users will quickly adapt to REWARDS as a result of its strict adherance to the macOS Design Guidelines.
